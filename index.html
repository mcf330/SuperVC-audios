Audio samples from SuperVC

Abstract: Previous works on Voice Conversion (VC) typically suffer from two primary issues: 1) incomplete disentanglement between speaker and linguistic information, leading to degradation of intelligibility and speaker similarity; 2) demand for external pretrained models for feature extraction, such as speaker verification models to provide speaker embedding or large language models to provide linguistic features. In this work, we present SuperVC, a novel end-to-end VC system aiming to overcome these problems. Firstly, we propose a novel supervised quantization process that effectively solves the decoupling problem. Secondly, by incorporating the idea of contrastive learning, we liberate the use of any external speaker embedding network and therefore obtain a fully end-to-end VC system with high robustness. The experimental result suggests that the proposed system is capable of producing conversion result that has high audio quality, and outstanding intelligibility and speaker similarity on both seen and unseen speakers.
